Step-by-Step: Your First Commute

  1. Plan your trip - Use our trip planner to know your route, transfers, and fare.
  2. Prepare payment - Have exact cash or a loaded Beep card ready.
  3. Enter the station - Look for the entrance with your line's sign.
  4. Buy ticket or tap Beep card - Go to the ticket booth or automated machine.
  5. Go through the gates - Tap your card or insert ticket, wait for the gate to open.
  6. Find your platform - Check signs for your direction (North/South or East/West).
  7. Wait behind the yellow line - Let passengers exit before boarding.
  8. Board the train - Move inside to make room for others.
  9. Listen for announcements - Count the stations to your destination.
  10. Alight and exit - Tap out with Beep card or insert ticket at the gate.
💡 First-Time Tip

If you're unsure, look for station staff in blue uniforms or tell other commuters it's your first time—many will help!

🎫 Tickets & Beep Card Guide

Option 1: Single Journey Ticket

Good for one trip on one line. Buy at ticket booths or vending machines.

  1. Check the fare - Use our fare calculator to know the amount.
  2. Go to the ticket booth - Tell the cashier your destination station.
  3. Pay exact fare - Cashiers may not have change for large bills.
  4. Get your ticket - It's a small plastic card with magnetic strip.
  5. Insert at entrance gate - Ticket pops out on top, take it.
  6. Keep your ticket - You'll need it to exit.
  7. Insert at exit gate - Gate keeps the ticket, you walk through.

Option 2: Beep Card (Recommended)

A reloadable card that works on MRT, LRT, and select buses. Saves time and gives small discounts.

  1. Buy a Beep card - At any station ticket booth. Cost: ₱100 (includes ₱80 load).
  2. Check your balance - Tap on the red scanner at station entrances.
  3. Reload when low - At ticket booths, vending machines, or convenience stores (7-Eleven, Ministop).
  4. Tap at entrance - Green light means success, red means insufficient balance.
  5. Tap at exit - Always tap out, or you'll be charged maximum fare next time.
💰 Beep Card Tips
  • Minimum load: ₱100 for new cards
  • Maximum load: ₱10,000
  • Keep card away from magnets and phones (can demagnetize)
  • Register your card at beep.com.ph to protect balance if lost
  • Student and senior discounts available with valid ID (must be verified at station)
⚠️ Common Mistakes
  • Not tapping out - You'll be charged maximum fare next time
  • Insufficient balance - Can't enter; reload at vending machines inside station
  • Losing your single journey ticket - You'll have to pay again to exit

Step-by-Step: Transferring at Cubao

  1. Alight at MRT-3 Araneta Center-Cubao - Follow signs to "Exit A" or "Farmers Plaza"
  2. Exit the paid area - Tap out with Beep card or insert ticket
  3. Walk through Farmers Plaza - Go straight, pass the food court
  4. Exit Farmers Plaza - Cross the street toward Gateway Mall
  5. Look for LRT-2 signs - Entrance is near Gateway Mall
  6. Enter LRT-2 station - Buy new ticket or tap Beep card
  7. Go to platform - Check if you need East (Antipolo) or West (Recto)
💡 Transfer Tips
  • You need a separate ticket or Beep card for each line (no free transfers)
  • Beep cards work on all lines - just tap in and out at each station
  • During rush hour, transfers can be very crowded - allow extra time
  • Follow the crowd - many commuters make these transfers daily
⚠️ Important

You cannot transfer without exiting and re-entering. You must pay separate fares for each line unless using a Beep card (still pays separately but no need to buy new ticket).

✨ Commuter Etiquette & Safety

Train Etiquette (The Unspoken Rules)

  • Let passengers exit first - Stand on the sides, not in front of the door
  • Move inside - Don't block the doors; move toward the center
  • Give up seats - For elderly, pregnant, persons with disabilities, and parents with children
  • No eating or drinking - Not allowed inside stations or trains
  • Keep volume low - Use headphones, speak quietly
  • No leaning on poles - Others need to hold them
  • Keep bags in front - Creates more space and prevents pickpocketing

Safety Tips

✅ DO
  • Keep valuables in front pockets
  • Be aware of your surroundings
  • Know your exit before arriving
  • Use designated waiting areas
  • Report suspicious items to security
❌ DON'T
  • Use phone near doors (snatching risk)
  • Fall asleep - you might miss your stop
  • Board overcrowded trains (wait for next)
  • Give money to strangers

Rush Hour Survival Guide

Peak hours: 7:00-9:00 AM and 5:00-8:00 PM (weekdays)

  1. Arrive early - Queues can be 15-30 minutes long
  2. Have payment ready - Beep card is fastest
  3. Position yourself wisely - First car is usually less crowded
  4. Let people off first - Otherwise no one can move
  5. Move to the center - Don't block doors
  6. Be patient - Everyone is trying to get to work/school too
🎒 What to Bring
  • Beep card or exact cash
  • Water (but don't drink inside train)
  • Hand sanitizer
  • Face mask (still required in some stations)
  • Phone with data (for our website!)

What to do if you get lost:

  1. Stay calm - Don't panic
  2. Ask station staff - Look for uniformed personnel
  3. Check our website - Use the trip planner to find your way back
  4. If you missed your stop - Stay on until the next station, then go to the opposite platform
  5. Lost item? - Go to the station's customer service counter

📚 Commuter Glossary

Beep Card

Reloadable card used for MRT, LRT, and buses. Tap at entrances and exits.

Single Journey Ticket

One-time use ticket for one line. Buy at station, return at exit.

Transfer

Changing from one line to another (e.g., MRT to LRT). Requires exiting and re-entering.

Platform

The area where you wait for the train. Has yellow safety line.

Alight

To get off the train at your station.
